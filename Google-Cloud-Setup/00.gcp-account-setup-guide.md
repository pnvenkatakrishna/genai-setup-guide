# 🌐 Google Cloud (GCP) – Complete Beginner Guide

## 📌 What is Google Cloud?

**Google Cloud** (GCP) is a cloud computing platform that provides services like:

* Virtual machines (servers)
* Storage (databases, object storage)
* Networking
* AI/ML tools
* DevOps & deployment tools
---

## 🎁 Free Tier Overview

GCP provides **two types of free usage**:

## 1. Free Trial (Best for Beginners)

* 💰 **$300 credits**
* ⏳ Valid for 90 days
* Access to almost all services

## 2. Always Free Tier

Some services are free every month (limited usage), like:

* Small VM instance (Compute Engine)
* 5 GB storage
* Cloud Functions (limited invocations)

---

## 📝 Prerequisites (India & Global)

Before creating an account:

* ✅ **Google account (Gmail)**
* 💳 **Debit/Credit card (international enabled)**
* 🌍 **Valid phone number**
* 📍 **Address (India or any country)**

👉 **Note:**

* No money is deducted for free trial (Only ₹2 will be deducted for verification — fully refundable.)
* Works globally, including India.

---

## 🚀 Step-by-Step: Create GCP Free Tier Account

## Step 1: Open Google Cloud Website

Go to:
👉 [https://cloud.google.com/](https://cloud.google.com/)

Click **“Get started for free”**

---

## Step 2: Login with Google Account

* Use existing Gmail OR create new one

---

## Step 3: Select Country & Accept Terms

* Choose: India (or your country)
* Accept terms and conditions

---

## Step 4: Billing Setup

You will be asked to:

* Enter card details
* Verify identity

### 💡 Important Notes:

* ₹2–₹10 temporary hold may appear
* It gets refunded automatically
*You won’t be charged unless you choose to **upgrade** or **activate the full account**.

---

## Step 5: Identity Verification

* OTP verification (phone)
* Address confirmation

---

## Step 6: Activate Free Trial

After verification:

* $300 credits activated
* Billing account created

---

## Step 7: Access Console

Open:
👉 [https://console.cloud.google.com/](https://console.cloud.google.com/)

This is your main dashboard.

---

## 💳 Payment Methods in Google Cloud (India)

When creating a Google Cloud account in India, you will typically see two payment options:

### 1. 💳 Card Payment (Recommended)

**Supported:**

* Credit Cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
* Debit Cards (International transactions enabled)

**How it works:**

* A small temporary charge (₹2–₹10 or ~$1) is applied for verification
* This amount is automatically refunded
* No AutoPay setup is required

**Advantages:**

* Simple and quick setup
* No confusion with billing limits
* Best option for beginners

---

### 2. 📱 UPI Payment (India Only)

**How it works:**

* Requires approval of an **AutoPay mandate**
* You may see a limit like **₹15,000**

---

### ⚠️ Important Clarification

> The ₹15,000 limit is **NOT a charge**.

It means:

* This is the **maximum amount Google is allowed to charge**
* It is required due to **RBI e-mandate rules for recurring payments**
* No money is deducted unless you actually use paid services

---

### 🧠 Key Differences

| Feature             | Card Payment            | UPI Payment              |
| ------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Verification        | Small refundable charge | AutoPay mandate required |
| AutoPay setup       | ❌ Not required          | ✅ Required               |
| Billing limit shown | ❌ No                    | ✅ ₹15,000 cap            |
| Beginner-friendly   | ✅ Yes                   | ⚠️ Can be confusing      |

---

### 🛑 When Will You Be Charged?

You will only be charged if:

* Your **$300 free trial credits are exhausted**
* You **upgrade your account manually**
* You use **paid services beyond free limits**

---

### ✅ Recommendation

> 💡 For beginners, it is strongly recommended to use a **credit/debit card** instead of UPI to avoid confusion with AutoPay mandates.

---

### 📌 Quick Summary

* ₹15,000 in UPI = **authorization limit, not deduction**
* Card payments = **simpler and safer for beginners**
* No charges unless you exceed free tier or upgrade billing
